# Use with ChatGPT, Claude.ai, or any AI

No installation required. Works right now.

## Steps

1. **Pick a skill** from the [catalog](../README.md#all-40-skills)
2. **Click the link** to open the SKILL.md file on GitHub
3. **Copy the entire content** (click the "Raw" button on GitHub, then Ctrl+A, Ctrl+C)
4. **Open your AI assistant** (ChatGPT, Claude.ai, Gemini, etc.)
5. **Paste the skill content** as your first message
6. **Type:** "Start the interview"
7. **The AI will adopt the interviewer persona** and begin Phase 1

## Tips

- **Use a capable model.** GPT-4, GPT-4o, Claude Sonnet/Opus, or Gemini Pro work best. Smaller models may not follow the persona consistently.
- **Say "I'd like a hint"** when stuck. The AI will give you Level 1 first, then escalate if you ask again.
- **At the end, say "Give me my scorecard"** to get your evaluation.
- **You can paste the references/problems.md file too** if you want the AI to have access to the full problem bank with walkthroughs.

## Example prompt after pasting

> Start the interview. I'm preparing for a senior backend engineer role and I'd rate my system design skills as intermediate.

## What to expect

- A warm-up question to calibrate your level (5 min)
- Deep dive into concepts with ASCII diagrams (15 min)
- A realistic problem with progressive hints (20 min)
- A scorecard with specific strengths and improvement areas (5 min)

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| AI doesn't adopt the persona | Make sure you pasted the *entire* SKILL.md including the YAML frontmatter at the top |
| AI gives answers too easily | Say "Don't give me the answer. Push me to think through it." |
| AI stops following the structure | Remind it: "Continue following the interview structure in the skill" |
| Session feels too short | Say "Let's do another problem from the problem bank" |
